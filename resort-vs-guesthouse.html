Resort vs. Guesthouse: Which Is Actually Better For You?

7 min read · The Maldives Guide

This is the first question almost every first-time visitor asks, and it doesn't have a universal answer — it depends what you actually want out of the trip. Here's the honest version, not the marketing version.

The core difference isn't price — it's the island itself

A resort occupies its own private island. You won't share it with anyone who isn't also a paying guest, and everything on it — the restaurants, the beach, the staff — exists purely for tourism. A guesthouse sits on an inhabited local island, alongside a real Maldivian community: shops, a mosque, families, school kids walking home. Price follows from that difference, but it isn't the starting point.

A resort sells you privacy. A guesthouse sells you a place that was never built around you in the first place — and that's exactly its appeal.

Where each one actually wins

Resorts win on:

  • Privacy — no one on the island isn't also a guest
  • Water villas and house reefs directly off your room
  • Alcohol availability
  • All-inclusive predictability, if that matters to you

Guesthouses win on:

  • Cost — often a third of a comparable resort
  • Real local food, not resort-restaurant food
  • Genuine culture and conversation, not a curated version of it
  • Flexibility — easy to switch islands mid-trip if you want to

The question we'd actually ask you

Not "what's your budget" first — but: do you want your trip to feel like an escape, or do you want it to feel like you actually visited a place? Neither answer is wrong. A honeymoon usually leans resort. A first trip to understand what the Maldives is actually like usually leans guesthouse, or both.
