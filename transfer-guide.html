Speedboat, seaplane, domestic flight, or ferry — what's actually the difference

The transfer is the single most confusing part of planning a Maldives trip for first-time visitors — and picking the wrong one can cost you money or a half-day of your holiday. Here's the plain version.

Side by side

Quick logic

How to think about which one you need

IFyour resort or guesthouse is close to Male (roughly under 1 hour by boat) → speedboat is almost always used, and it's the cheapest option relative to distance.
IFyour flight lands after sunset → seaplane isn't an option that night. You'll need a speedboat-accessible property, or an overnight in Male before flying out the next morning.
IFyour resort is in a remote atoll (like Addu or Fuvahmulah) → it's usually a domestic flight plus a short boat ride, not a seaplane.
IFyou're staying on an inhabited local island for your guesthouse trip → it's public ferry (cheapest, limited schedule) or a shared speedboat (faster, a bit more).

Avoid these

Common transfer mistakes

Booking a resort without checking transfer cost

A cheap-looking resort rate can be offset entirely by a $500+ per-person seaplane transfer. Always ask for the all-in total, not just the room rate.

Forgetting seaplanes don't fly at night

If your international flight lands late, a seaplane-only resort may mean an unplanned night in Male first.

Underestimating the wait

Seaplane transfers often involve waiting at a lounge for your flight group to fill — build padding into your day-one plans rather than expecting to arrive immediately.
