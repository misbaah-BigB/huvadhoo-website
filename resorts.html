Resorts

Six ways to do a Maldives resort — matched to what you actually want

Every resort occupies its own private island, so the difference between categories isn't just price — it's distance from Male, house reef quality, and what's included. We match you to the category first, then the specific resort.

Categories

Pick a starting point

These are ranges, not fixed prices — your actual quote depends on dates, room type, and availability.

Budget-luxury

Budget-Luxury Resort

A real private-island resort experience at the lowest resort price point — smaller rooms, simpler dining, but still your own island.

Best for: first resort trip, longer stays on a set budget $180 – $350 / night
All-inclusive

All-Inclusive Resort

Meals, drinks, and often excursions bundled into one price — the most predictable total cost of any resort category.

Best for: predictable budgeting, families, first-time visitors $350 – $700 / night
Family

Family Resort

Real kids' clubs, shallow lagoons safe for swimming, and family-configured villas — not just a regular resort with a crib added.

Best for: children under 12, multi-generation trips $300 – $900 / night
Honeymoon

Honeymoon & Adults-Only Resort

Water villas, private dining, and a resort culture genuinely built around couples — quiet, not family-oriented.

Best for: honeymoons, anniversaries, couples-only trips $450 – $1,800 / night
Premium

Premium 5-Star Resort

Top-tier service and dining with a larger footprint — multiple restaurants, spa, watersports center, often a longer transfer.

Best for: milestone trips, longer stays, strong reef access $600 – $1,400 / night
Ultra-luxury

Private-Island Ultra-Luxury

Full villa privacy, butler service, and the highest-end dining — the top of the Maldives resort market.

Best for: privacy above all, special occasions, no budget ceiling $1,200 – $6,000+ / night
