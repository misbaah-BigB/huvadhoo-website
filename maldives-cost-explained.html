How Much Does a Maldives Trip Really Cost?

6 min read · The Maldives Guide

The Maldives has a reputation as one of the most expensive destinations on earth. That's true if you're only looking at 5-star resort brochures — it's not true of the Maldives as a whole. Here's what a real trip costs, by style.

Five real trip budgets

Trip stylePer night (2 people)5-night total, all-in
Guesthouse, budget$60–$120$450–$850
Guesthouse, comfortable$120–$180$850–$1,300
Resort + Local Combo$180–$500 blended$1,100–$2,900
Mid-range resort$300–$700$2,000–$4,200
Luxury / private-island resort$800–$3,000+$4,500–$18,000+

These figures already include the typical taxes, service charge, and transfer for that trip style — not just the room rate, which is the number that misleads most people researching online.

The three costs everyone forgets to budget for

  • The transfer. Can range from $10 (public ferry) to over $500 per person (seaplane, round trip). It's rarely included in the headline room price you see advertised.
  • Taxes and service charge. Typically add up to roughly a quarter of your room cost on top — green tax, GST, and service charge combined.
  • Meal plan upgrades. The jump from breakfast-only to half-board or all-inclusive can add $80–$300 per person for a 5-night stay.
A "cheap" resort rate with a seaplane transfer often ends up costing more than a slightly pricier resort that's a short speedboat ride away.

How to actually save money without a worse trip

  1. Pick a resort or guesthouse reachable by speedboat, not seaplane, if budget matters more than remoteness.
  2. Consider the Resort + Local Island Combo — it brings your blended nightly average down while still giving you real resort nights.
  3. Travel shoulder season (roughly April–May or October–November) for lower resort rates without peak monsoon weather.
  4. Ask for the full all-in total before comparing prices between two properties — comparing room-only rates is comparing different things.
