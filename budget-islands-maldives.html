Best Maldives Islands for Budget Travelers

8 min read · The Maldives Guide

Not every local island feels the same. Some are lively and built for first-timers, others are quiet and closer to how the Maldives felt before tourism arrived. Here are six worth knowing.

Maafushi — South Male Atoll

The most established guesthouse island in the country. The widest choice of restaurants, excursions, and guesthouses — a safe, easy pick for a first Maldives trip.

Guraidhoo — South Male Atoll

Quieter than Maafushi with a similar bikini beach setup, and close to some genuinely good snorkeling sites.

Dhigurah — Alifu Dhaalu Atoll

Known for one of the longest natural beaches in the country, and for regular whale shark sightings nearby — a strong pick if marine life is the priority.

Fulidhoo — Vaavu Atoll

Small and slow-paced, known for Bodu Beru cultural drumming evenings. A good choice if you want something quieter than Maafushi.

Dharavandhoo — Baa Atoll

Close to Hanifaru Bay, one of the world's most reliable manta ray snorkeling sites during season — worth the slightly longer trip from Male.

Gan — Laamu Atoll

Further out but connected by causeway to several neighboring islands, giving you more to explore on foot. Quieter and less touristy than the South Male islands.
