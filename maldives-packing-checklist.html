Maldives Packing Checklist

4 min read · The Maldives Guide

A tropical island trip doesn't need a complicated packing list — but a few Maldives-specific items are easy to forget until you're already there.

Sun and swim

  • Reef-safe sunscreen — many resorts and dive operators now require it to protect coral
  • A rash guard or light long-sleeve top for snorkeling, to cut down on reapplying sunscreen
  • A wide-brim hat and good sunglasses — the sun is stronger than it feels over water

If you're staying on a local island

  • A lightweight cover-up or sarong for walking between the bikini beach and the rest of the island
  • Modest clothing (shoulders and knees covered) for anywhere off the designated beach

Tech

  • A universal adapter — the Maldives generally uses UK-style (type G) plugs at 230V
  • A waterproof phone pouch for boat transfers and beach days
  • A portable charger — outlets can be limited on some local islands

Health and comfort

  • Basic over-the-counter medication, including something for seasickness if you're prone to it — boat transfers can get choppy
  • A light jacket or wrap for air-conditioned rooms and evening sea breezes

Documents

  • Passport with the validity your airline/entry requirements call for — check current requirements before you fly
  • A printed or downloaded copy of your booking confirmation
  • Your completed IMUGA immigration form — it's free, done online, and required before arrival
The one thing not to pack: your own alcohol, if any part of your trip includes a local island stay — it can be confiscated at customs.
