Speedboat vs. Seaplane: The Simple Explanation

4 min read · The Maldives Guide

This is the question we get asked more than almost any other. The short version: it depends entirely on how far your resort is from Male, not on price or luxury tier.

The one-line version

Close to Male (roughly under an hour by boat) → speedboat. Farther out → seaplane, or sometimes a domestic flight plus a short boat ride.

Speedboat

  • Cheaper — typically $40–$150 per person round trip
  • Runs day or night
  • 15–90 minutes depending on distance
  • Can get choppy in rough weather, worth knowing if you're prone to seasickness

Seaplane

  • More expensive — typically $300–$550 per person round trip
  • Daylight hours only — no exceptions
  • 25–45 minute flight, with genuinely stunning aerial views of the atolls
  • Often involves a wait at the seaplane lounge for your group's flight to be ready
Seaplanes don't fly after dark. If your international flight lands in the evening, a seaplane-only resort isn't reachable that night — check this before you book.

What about domestic flights and public ferries?

Domestic flight + boat is common for resorts in far-out atolls like Addu or Fuvahmulah, where a seaplane wouldn't be practical. Public ferries are the budget option for reaching inhabited local islands — slower and less frequent, but a fraction of the cost of a speedboat.
