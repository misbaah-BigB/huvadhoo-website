Guide

What actually goes into your total Maldives price

Taxes, green tax, service charge, and meal plans catch first-time visitors off guard because they're often not shown until checkout. Here's every line item explained, and a worked example of what a real total looks like.

Worked example

A real total price, broken down

5 nights, 2 adults, budget-luxury resort, half-board, speedboat transfer — illustrative figures.

Sample 5-night quote

Room rate (5 nights)$1,250

Line by line

What each cost actually is

Green tax

A flat government environmental fee charged per person, per night — typically higher for resorts than guesthouses. It's mandatory and separate from the room rate.

Service charge

Usually around 10%, added by the resort or guesthouse itself. Not always negotiable, and rarely optional despite the name.

GST / tourism tax

A government tax on tourism goods and services, typically in the mid-teens percentage-wise, applied on top of the room rate.

Meal plan

Ranges from room-only to full all-inclusive. This is often the single biggest cost swing in your total — see the comparison below.

Watch for these

What's often not included by default

Wi-Fi

Free at most resorts now, but still charged at some budget properties — we confirm this before booking.

Excursions

Diving, sandbank trips, and sunset cruises are almost always priced separately from your room rate.

Alcohol at guesthouses

Not available at all on local islands, by law — not a pricing issue, but a real difference from resort budgeting.

Single supplement

Solo travelers often pay a surcharge on double-occupancy room rates — we'll flag this upfront if it applies to your booking.
