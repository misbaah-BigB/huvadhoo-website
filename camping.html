Island camping

Sleep on an uninhabited island — done legally, safely, and properly permitted

Camping on an uninhabited Maldives island is a genuinely special experience — but it requires official permission and a licensed operator. We handle every permit and never arrange unauthorized "wild camping."

Permitted trips only

Uninhabited islands in the Maldives require official permission to visit and camp overnight. We only arrange trips through licensed operators who secure the correct permits — unauthorized camping isn't something we offer, at any price.

What's included

What a permitted trip actually provides

Permits, handled for you

We arrange the required permission to visit and camp on the specific island — not something you need to sort out yourself.

Gear

Tents, sleeping equipment, and basic camp setup are provided by the operator — you don't need to bring your own camping gear.

Food and water

Meals and drinking water are typically included and prepared on-site or brought over — confirmed in your quote before you go.

Safety and transfer

A licensed boat handles transfer to and from the island, with safety equipment and a guide who knows the specific island's conditions.
