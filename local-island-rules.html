Guide

Local island rules, in plain language — read this before you book a guesthouse

Inhabited local islands follow Maldivian law and Islamic custom — genuinely different from a private resort island. None of it should surprise you on arrival. Here's exactly what to expect.

The essentials

What's different from a resort

🍻

Alcohol

Not sold or permitted on local islands, by law. It's available at resorts and on licensed liveaboards, but not on inhabited islands — no exceptions, including bringing your own.

👙

Swimwear

Bikinis and swim trunks are fine on the island's designated "bikini beach." Elsewhere on the island, modest dress (shoulders and knees covered) is expected of both women and men.

🌙

Ramadan

During Ramadan, visible eating, drinking, or smoking in public during daylight hours is discouraged out of respect — restaurants may adjust hours, and it's worth planning around this if traveling during the holy month.

🐕

Pets and pork

Pork products aren't available on local islands, in line with Islamic dietary law. Dogs are uncommon and not typically kept as pets locally.

🕌

Friday prayer time

Some shops and services pause briefly around midday on Fridays for prayer — a minor scheduling quirk worth knowing, not a disruption to your trip.

📸

Public affection

Public displays of affection between couples are generally discouraged on local islands, more so than the resort norm.

Worth saying plainly

This isn't a restriction on your holiday — it's a different kind of holiday

Guesthouse travelers consistently describe local islands as warm, welcoming, and genuinely interesting — real conversations, real food, a real sense of place that a resort simply can't offer. The rules above are easy to plan around once you know them, and most visitors barely think about them past day one.
