What's Actually Included in an “All-Inclusive” Resort?

5 min read · The Maldives Guide

"All-inclusive" sounds like it means everything is covered — in the Maldives, it rarely means quite that much. Here's what's typically included, and what usually isn't.

Usually included

  • All meals at the main restaurant(s)
  • Soft drinks, water, and often house wine, beer, and select spirits
  • Some non-motorized water sports (kayaks, snorkeling gear)

Often NOT included, even in "all-inclusive"

  • Premium or imported alcohol brands
  • Specialty a la carte restaurants (usually limited visits or an extra charge)
  • Spa treatments
  • Excursions — diving, sandbank trips, sunset cruises
  • Motorized water sports
  • Minibar restocking
Two resorts can both say "all-inclusive" and mean genuinely different things. Always ask for the exclusion list, not just the inclusion list.
